I run a decision pipeline that processes information signals
and routes them through gates before any action is taken.

DETECTION — identifying weak signals before they become
  obvious. Separating real signals from noise. Classifying

ADMISSION — filtering signals through quality gates.
  Does the signal have a specific mechanism? Does it survive
  adversarial stress-testing? What is the probability it is
  wrong?

SCORING — computing an edge score across 22+ weighted
  factors. The weights are currently designed priors —
  not empirically calibrated. I need a recalibration
  mechanism.

ROUTING — deciding how much to commit based on signal
  quality, regime, and expected duration. Not binary.
  Graduated exposure: full / half / quarter or nothing.

DECAY MODELLING — how long does a signal of type X persist
  before it fades? Currently modelled as AR(1) with a
  decay coefficient φ per signal type. Not yet empirically
  fitted.

STATE TRANSITIONS — tracking when a signal moves from
  forming → confirmed → crowded → fragile → broken.
  Six states. Transition thresholds are qualitative.

LEARNING — after every closed decision, what did I get
  wrong? How do I update the weights? How do I avoid
  over-updating on noise vs under-updating on real errors?

What I need from the Harvard Advanced Algorithms material:
theorems, proofs, data structures, or algorithmic patterns
that make any of these seven layers more rigorous, more
precise, or more correctly specified.
