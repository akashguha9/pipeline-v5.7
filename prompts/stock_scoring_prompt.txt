THE SCORING SYSTEM — 7 LAYERS WITH WEIGHTS

Score each layer 0–100. The weighted total is the final score.

LAYER 1 — SURVIVAL & LIQUIDITY (Weight: 20%)
This is the floor. If a company fails here, score the entire stock AVOID regardless of other layers. Do not average through a failure here.

Variables to score (all equally weighted within layer):
- Current Ratio: optimal 1.8–2.5. Below 1.2 = red flag.
- Cash Ratio: optimal 0.5–1.0.
- OCF vs Net Income: OCF must be ≥ Net Income over 3 years. If not → manipulation risk, cap Layer 1 at 40.
- Cash Conversion Cycle: shorter over time = better. Rising CCC = deteriorating efficiency.
- Interest Coverage (EBIT/Interest): must be >5x. Below 2x = AVOID regardless of everything else.
- Fixed Cost Ratio: target below 50–60% of total cost base.

Scoring guide:
- 80–100: all metrics in optimal range, no red flags
- 60–79: most metrics healthy, 1 minor concern
- 40–59: mixed — some stress indicators present
- Below 40: survival risk present → AVOID trigger

---

LAYER 2 — CAPITAL EFFICIENCY & RETURNS (Weight: 20%)
The engine quality check. ROIC vs WACC is the single most important number.

Variables:
- ROIC vs WACC: ROIC must exceed WACC by at least 5–8%. Below WACC = value destruction.
- ROE: optimal 15–25%. Above 25% check if it is leverage-driven.
- Debt-to-Equity: optimal 0.5–1.0. Above 2.0 = concern.
- Asset Turnover: trend should increase. Declining = efficiency erosion.
- Working Capital Turnover: CCC should shorten over time.
- FCF Conversion (FCF/Net Income): must be >80%. Below 60% = quality concern.
- Earnings Quality Ratio (OCF/Net Income): must be ≥1.0. Below 0.8 = manipulation territory.
- Sustainable Growth Rate (ROE × Retention Ratio): growth above SGR requires leverage or dilution.

Scoring guide:
- 80–100: ROIC beats WACC by 8%+, FCF conversion >90%, earnings quality ≥1.1
- 60–79: ROIC beats WACC by 5–8%, FCF conversion 80–90%, minor concerns
- 40–59: ROIC barely beats WACC or unclear, FCF conversion 60–80%
- Below 40: ROIC < WACC or earnings quality <0.8 → cap total score, lean AVOID

---

LAYER 3 — PROFITABILITY QUALITY (Weight: 15%)
Margins must be real, growing, and above industry peers.

Variables:
- Gross Margin: target top quartile of industry. Score vs peers.
- Gross Margin Premium vs Industry: target +5–15% above average.
- EBITDA Margin: only as comparative metric. Use alongside OCF.
- Net Profit Margin: services 15–25%, products 10–20%, asset-heavy 8–15%. Below 5% = weak buffer.
- Operating Leverage: high leverage with stable demand → profit inevitability. Unstable demand → amplified risk.
- Break-Even Cushion: target 30–40% revenue above break-even.

Scoring guide:
- 80–100: margins expanding, above peers, break-even cushion >35%
- 60–79: stable margins, in-line with peers, some expansion
- 40–59: margins flat or slightly compressing, near peer average
- Below 40: margins declining, below peers, or break-even cushion <15%

---

LAYER 4 — GROWTH QUALITY (Weight: 15%)
Revenue growth means nothing if earnings do not follow. Organic growth beats acquired growth.

Variables:
- Revenue Growth Rate: target 10–20% annually. Above 30% — check if sustainable.
- Earnings Growth Rate: must track revenue growth. Revenue up + earnings flat = illusion.
- Organic Revenue Growth Rate: exclude acquisitions. This is real heat.
- FCF Growth: must trend positive alongside revenue.
- Reinvestment Rate: only reinvest if ROIC >20%. Otherwise return capital.
- Marginal ROIC: return on last dollar invested. If marginal ROIC > historical ROIC → acceleration phase.
- R&D Intensity: tech/high innovation 10–20%, traditional 3–6%.
- % Revenue from New Products (last 3 years): optimal 20–40%. Below 10% = stagnation risk.
- Incremental Gross Profit: target >1.5–2.0 over 3 years. Below 1.0 = innovation theater.

Scoring guide:
- 80–100: revenue AND earnings both growing 15%+, organic, FCF follows, marginal ROIC accelerating
- 60–79: solid growth, earnings roughly tracking revenue, some acquisition noise
- 40–59: growth slowing or earnings not following revenue
- Below 40: revenue growing but earnings flat, or declining across the board

---

LAYER 5 — MOAT & PRICING POWER (Weight: 15%)
Is this company genuinely hard to displace? Does it price above competitors without losing volume?

Variables:
- Recurring Revenue Ratio: >70% = structural inevitability. ~50% = strong.
- Revenue Backlog Coverage: backlog/next 12 months revenue. >0.7 = high predictability.
- NRR (Net Revenue Retention): >110% good, >120% = strong expansion economics.
- Customer Concentration: top 5 customers <30–40% of revenue.
- Switching Cost Indicator: proxy via retention rate, contract lock-ins, integration CapEx by client.
- ROIC Stability (5yr std deviation): low volatility = structural moat.
- Gross Margin Premium: if "cool" but margins are average → brand is not translating.
- Price Realization Rate (actual price / list price): close to 1.0 = strong demand. Discounting = weakness.
- ASP Growth: sustained ASP growth without volume collapse = real pricing power.
- CAC Trend: if brand is strong, CAC should decline over time. Rising CAC = buying attention, not earning it.
- Inventory Sell-Through Rate: high sell-through = no forced discounting. (Retail/consumer only.)

Scoring guide:
- 80–100: recurring revenue >60%, NRR >115%, ROIC stable, ASP growing, CAC declining
- 60–79: recurring >40%, NRR >100%, some moat evidence
- 40–59: mixed moat — switching costs moderate, retention acceptable
- Below 40: no visible moat, commoditised pricing, CAC rising

---

LAYER 6 — INEVITABILITY & POWER PROXIMITY (Weight: 10%)
Can this company access cheap capital and maintain strategic position regardless of market cycles?

Variables:
- WACC vs Competitors: lower WACC = power proximity advantage. Institutional trust lowers cost.
- Debt Spread vs Benchmark: tighter spread = stronger institutional credibility.
- Institutional Ownership %: healthy 30–70% in public firms.
- Equity Access: historical dilution %, market appetite during capital raises.
- Regulatory Barrier Index: CapEx required to enter industry. High barrier = power insulation.
- Government Revenue Exposure: stability vs political dependency — context-dependent.
- Operating Leverage with Stable Demand: high leverage + stable = profit inevitability.

Scoring guide:
- 80–100: WACC demonstrably below peers, institutional ownership 40–65%, low dilution history
- 60–79: reasonable capital access, moderate institutional ownership
- 40–59: limited capital advantage, higher dilution history
- Below 40: expensive capital, low institutional trust, or high political dependency

---

LAYER 7 — POTENTIAL & OPTIONALITY (Weight: 5%)
Runway matters. But only if ROIC supports reinvestment.

Variables:
- TAM Penetration %: low penetration + strong unit economics = runway.
- Marginal ROIC > Historical ROIC: acceleration phase — still improving.
- Capital Optionality (cash + untapped credit / total assets): ability to deploy quickly.
- Reinvestment Runway: how many high-ROIC projects remain before marginal returns decline.
- R&D Pipeline Value (NPV): discounted expected value of innovation projects.
- Real Options Value: expansion rights, geographic scalability, platform extensibility.

Scoring guide:
- 80–100: TAM <15% penetrated, marginal ROIC accelerating, capital optionality >20% of assets
- 60–79: moderate runway, stable reinvestment opportunity
- 40–59: limited runway or ROIC already peaking
- Below 40: growth requires dilution or leverage, no obvious reinvestment edge

VANITY CHECK (runs after all layers — automatic disqualifier if 3+ of these are true):
- Revenue growth > cash flow growth for 3+ years
- Marketing spend rising but gross margin declining
- EV multiple expanding but ROIC declining
- Follower/user growth ≠ revenue growth
- EBITDA margin strong but OCF consistently weaker
→ If 3+ vanity signals present: flag as NARRATIVE INFLATION RISK. Cap total score at 60 regardless of other layers.
