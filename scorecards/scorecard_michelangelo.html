SOURCE_PURITY_FACTOR — Three Independent Failure Modes
SPF — Strategic Distortion
How much does this source INTENTIONALLY distort? Activist PR, government spin, sports agent claims, echo chambers. Updates: slow (baseline characterization).
0.0 = Polymarket mechanics (near-zero)
0.5 = institutional research (incentive structure)
1.0 = known manipulator / spin narrative
SCS — Domain Competence
Does this source UNDERSTAND the relevant domain? Out-of-domain celebrity vs deep domain expert. Updates: moderate (expertise relatively stable).
0.0 = celebrity tweeting geopolitics
0.5 = adjacent domain proximity
1.0 = deep expert with track record
SAS — Accuracy Track Record
Empirical hit rate on THIS signal class. Computed from Moltbook outcomes. Updates: FAST (per closed trade). The declining-SAS pattern is the most dangerous.
0.0 = below chance on similar predictions
0.5 = random noise baseline
1.0 = significantly above base rate

SOURCE_PURITY_FACTOR Architecture (SPF / SCS / SAS)
Formally decomposes source trustworthiness into three independent dimensions. The pipeline has SOURCE_BUBBLE_SCORE (SBS) which measures distortion but as one composite. SPF separates three distinct failure modes with different operational responses and different update rates. T_i = (1−SPF_s) × SCS_s × SAS_s replaces single-score distortion_prior with a three-factor product. SPF: strategic distortion tendency (slow updates — baseline characterization). SCS: domain competence (moderate updates — expertise is stable). SAS: empirical accuracy on THIS signal class (FAST updates — computed from Moltbook per closed trade). The Assamese proverb insight operationalized: "excessive devotion = suspicious" because cost is low and consistency is low (SPF=HIGH, SCS may be irrelevant, SAS=unknown). "Children speak truth" operationalized: low SPF, variable SCS, variable SAS — high purity, uncertain competence, uncertain accuracy.
